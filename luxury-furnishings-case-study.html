01: Problem & Goals

The Gap Between
Brand & Experience

Luxora was a beloved high-end furniture brand with loyal showroom clients, but their e-commerce site told a completely different story. Cart abandonment sat at 71%, and the product experience was generic enough to belong to any budget retailer.

My mandate: redesign product discovery and checkout to match the brand's luxury positioning. As an interior designer, I understood exactly what this audience needed, and what they'd reject.

02: Research & Discovery

What Luxury Shoppers
Actually Need

I analyzed Luxora heatmaps and session recordings, then conducted 12 interviews with high-intent luxury furniture shoppers. The pattern was clear: they needed to be seduced before they wanted specs.

"When I'm spending this much on a sofa, I need to see it in a room that looks like mine. And I need to feel like the brand understands taste, not just products."

— Interview participant, 44, homeowner
01
Inspire First
Lead with editorial photography and room context before product specs enter the picture.
02
Inform Second
Material, provenance, and craftsmanship details build conviction, once the user is emotionally engaged.
03
Transact Third
The checkout is a commitment ceremony, not a form. Make it feel effortless and reassuring.

Key insight from competitive benchmarking: luxury buyers are conviction shoppers, not comparison shoppers. The job of the UX is to build conviction, not optimize for speed.

03: Process & Wireframes

Designing for
Conviction

I restructured the entire site around the principle: inspire first, inform second, transact third. Every screen was evaluated against this order: if it didn't inspire first, it didn't belong at the top of the experience.

04: Final Design

The Showroom,
Digitized

The final design gave Luxora a digital experience that finally matched their physical brand: unhurried, beautiful, and completely confident in its own taste level. My interior design background was the unfair advantage here: I understood what high-end furniture buyers care about in a way a purely digital designer might have missed.

"For the first time, our website feels like us. Customers are spending longer on product pages and asking fewer pre-purchase questions."

— Head of Digital, Luxora

Where Luxury Meets
Conversion

Three months post-launch, the redesign delivered improvement across every tracked metric, and qualitative signals suggested an equally significant brand perception shift.
